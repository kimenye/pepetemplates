Dear Friends and clients,

This week I write a very personal letter to all of you. I write with peace and love in my heart as I have managed to remove the angst and fear of last week thanks to a very special healer called Hanna.

Nairobi has had a more horrific week than the majority of humanity can imagine, nor should they imagine it. I have managed to resurface with great energy and a determination to use my creativity to build a better tomorrow.

This is not business as usual in Kenya. As employers, piers, parents, etc we need to be proactive and help those around us. Let's start with ourselves first, then we can be helpful to others.

We cannot pretend it didn't happen, as we will be a nation that lives in fear. We also must not perpetuate the hurt and fear by looking at photos or sharing articles. We must have respect for our inner selves and that of those around us. We must use creativity and positivity to overcome such negative sentiments and bring our society together.

I want to be part of building a better tomorrow for Kenya, Africa and the Globe. You can help wherever you are. Bombings and incursions are not the answer, they just create more injustice and more hate. I am already in conversations with people of different faiths to see how we focus on the boy child, particularly the Muslim and immigrant boy child (in all countries of the world). When we marginalise them they become tomorrow's problem. They are often rebellious teenagers, who don’t quite fit in, recent converts with no family reference points or boys who are desperate for independence and their own money. Very many have no idea of what they are getting involved in until it’s too late. They are on a one-way ticket and brainwashed. We need to include them in society, approach them with love, and listen to their problems, before others with bad intentions do that first.

I want to use sports and arts to keep our boys safe & change perceptions one mind at a time. KikoRomeo will now look at how it can help, FAFA will hold Fashion for Peace on 2nd & 3rd of November and I as an individual in society will see what I can do, to save our boys. KikoRomeo will also hold a show this Wednesday as we set about reclaiming our spaces, conquering fear, and living our lives.

#WeAreOne Join me.

Ann McCreath
Founder & MD KikoRomeo
Founder & Chair FAFA & Fashion for Peace
Board Member Kilimani Project Foundation.
